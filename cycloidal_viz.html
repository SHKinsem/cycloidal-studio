Reading it: tight at the mid-flank (where the arm is longest) carries load with little lost motion — that buys stiffness and low backlash. Relieved at tip and root leaves room for the tolerance stack and softens entry/exit shock. The GA optimum is that reverse-bow, sitting right at the tolerance edge (margin 0.68′) to keep four teeth loaded.
